**Column definitions:**
- **Safety Gate**: Has a binary mechanism to block AI participation (shield, verifier, safety monitor, backup switch)
- **Adaptive Autonomy**: Changes governance mode or autonomy level at runtime based on risk or operational context
- **AI Participation Controlled (L1)**: Formal mechanism governing *when* AI is allowed to participate — G(S) or equivalent
- **Advisory Scope Restricted (L2)**: Formal mechanism governing *what* AI is allowed to recommend — A_AI(S) or equivalent
- **Unified Governance**: Both L1 and L2 implemented as a formally unified pair conditioned on the same classified state variable
- **Formal Model**: Has a mathematical model with a proved or formally stated safety property

**Reading the table**: Every prior study either lacks adaptive autonomy (rows 1–8, 20–23, plus OWASP, Feng, IoT papers), lacks advisory scope restriction (Flehmig, Kwon — despite being environment-aware and runtime-adaptive), lacks unified governance (Baxi — scope exists but conditioned on agent robustness not environmental state), or lacks a formal model. This Research is the only entry with all six columns Yes. Kwon et al. (2025) is the closest near-miss: environment-centric features + runtime adaptation + formal guarantees, but SafetyScore still binary. Feng et al. (2025) reveals the design-time vs runtime distinction: autonomy levels are fixed deployment-time constants, not runtime state-conditioned. The four IoT papers (Chandran, Zhang, Ogenyi, Tandel) confirm that environmental sensors exist but are used as AI input, never as governance triggers. OWASP (2025) establishes global industry consensus on binary governance for agentic AI.
