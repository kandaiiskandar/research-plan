## Traceability Notes

### Formal Architecture Pipeline
The complete governance pipeline formalised in Appendix C and represented in the architecture diagram:

**E → S = f(E) → (G(S), A_AI(S)) → AI(E)**

Where the AI decision system is governed by the pair (G(S), A_AI(S)), constituting a two-level governance structure.

### Novelty Thread
- **PS1 → PS2** establishes the primary contribution: a two-level governance architecture consisting of participation governance G(S) and advisory scope governance A_AI(S), conditioned on environmental safety state S = f(E).
- **PS3** grounds it in the deployment domain (low-resource coastal fisheries).
- **PS4–PS5** ensure the CAUTION mode — the core novelty — is directly tested both technically (PS4) and socio-technically (PS5).

### Two-Level Governance Mapping to Evaluation
- **Level 1 — Participation governance G(S):** Present in both the proposed architecture and the binary-gated baseline. Evaluated by comparing binary-gated vs. ungated baseline (O4).
- **Level 2 — Advisory scope governance A_AI(S):** Present only in the proposed architecture. Evaluated by comparing three-mode vs. binary-gated baseline (O4). This comparison isolates the contribution of Level 2.
- **CAUTION mode:** The state where Level 2 does its work — G(S) = 1 (same as SAFE at Level 1) but A_AI(CAUTION) ⊂ A_AI(SAFE) (different at Level 2). Socio-technical evaluation (O5) focuses on user response to this state, including how users interpret the restricted advisory scope.

### Key Alignment Checks
- Every objective explicitly requires the three-mode architecture; none can be satisfied by a binary gate.
- O2 names the formal property A_AI(SAFE) ⊃ A_AI(CAUTION) ⊃ A_AI(UNSAFE) = ∅ and the two-level governance structure, anchoring the formalisation to the gap.
- O4 introduces a three-condition baseline comparison (ungated, binary-gated, two-level graduated), directly testing whether Level 2 governance adds value beyond Level 1 alone.
- O4 methodology includes verification of the Safety Dominance Property: AI(E) ⊆ A_AI(S).
- O5 isolates the CAUTION state as a distinct evaluation target, explicitly asking how users interpret the restricted advisory scope, connecting the user study to the specific architectural contribution of Level 2 governance.

### Primary Comparators Referenced
- **Flehmig et al. (Implementing AI in SCS):** Closest prior art; three-level traffic-light model. Differentiated on: governance trigger (AI performance vs. environmental state), governance target (monitoring intensity vs. AI-admissible recommendation space), formal specification (weighted index vs. E → S = f(E) → (G(S), A_AI(S))), and governance structure (single-level vs. two-level).
- **Shamsujjoha et al. (Swiss Cheese Model):** Most comprehensive guardrail taxonomy; no AI participation mode dimension; no Level 2 advisory scope governance.
- **Li et al. (2026) — PSE-DRL:** Dynamic risk threshold with safe action correction; single-agent, no environmental state classification into discrete governance modes; no two-level governance separation.
- **Könighofer et al. (2025) — Shields:** Representative binary-gated baseline used in O4 comparison.
